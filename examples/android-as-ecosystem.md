# Example: Android as an Open Ecosystem

Android demonstrates both the power and the limits of partial openness.

---

## Mapping Android to AAA

### Core Loop
```
AOSP fork → OEM customization → Market selection → Ecosystem convergence
```

### Three Primitives
- **Access** — AOSP is publicly available
- **Mutation** — anyone can fork and modify (Amazon Fire OS, Chinese ROMs)
- **Recursion** — some changes flow back to AOSP; compatibility ensures reintegration

---

## What Android Gets Right

1. **Massive diversity** — thousands of device variants
2. **Global adaptation** — local markets customize the experience
3. **Layered openness** — core is open; Google services are optional
4. **Market selection** — consumers choose among variants

---

## What Android Demonstrates About AAA

### The Compatibility Tension

Android's strength (diversity) is also its weakness (fragmentation).

Too much divergence without merge → ecosystem splits.
Too much merge without divergence → innovation dies.

The solution is **compatibility protocols** — standards that allow independent evolution while preserving the ability to reintegrate.

### The Platform Trap

Google maintains control through:
- Google Play Services (closed)
- Compatibility Definition Document (compliance)
- OEM agreements (contracts)

This is the platform model masquerading as an open system.

---

## Lesson for AAA

Openness is not binary. It is a **gradient**.

The question is not "open or closed?" but:

> **Which parts of the system are open to evolution, and which are constrained — and why?**

A AAA is honest about this gradient. A platform pretends it doesn't exist.
