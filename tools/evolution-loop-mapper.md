# Tool: Evolution Loop Bottleneck Mapper

> Find exactly where your system's evolution is stuck.

---

## How to use

Take the last 3-5 changes attempted in your system. For each, trace it through the four stages of the evolution loop. The stage where most attempts die is your bottleneck.

---

## The Mapper

```
Change attempt: _________________________________

FORK — Was divergence possible?
□ Yes, easily
□ Yes, with difficulty
□ No — BLOCKED HERE ←

EXPERIMENT — Was the variant tested/tried?
□ Yes, thoroughly
□ Yes, partially
□ No — BLOCKED HERE ←

SELECTION — Was there a clear decision about the variant?
□ Yes, meritocratic (best idea won)
□ Yes, but political/arbitrary
□ No decision was made — BLOCKED HERE ←

MERGE — Was the successful variant integrated?
□ Yes, smoothly
□ Yes, with significant friction
□ No — BLOCKED HERE ←
```

Repeat for 3-5 change attempts.

---

## Pattern Analysis

| Bottleneck Pattern | Underlying Problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Always at FORK | Access problem. People can't diverge. | Open entry. Reduce barriers. Publish documentation. |
| Always at EXPERIMENT | Mutation problem. Can't test ideas. | Create safe experimentation spaces. Reduce cost of failure. |
| Always at SELECTION | Governance problem. No clear decision process. | Define selection criteria. Make them transparent. Diversify decision-makers. |
| Always at MERGE | Recursion problem. Can't integrate change. | Build merge infrastructure. Define compatibility standards. Reduce integration friction. |
| Random bottlenecks | General dysfunction. | Start with Access — it's the foundation. |

---

## Example

**System**: A corporate innovation program.

Change attempt 1 (new product idea): Fork ✅ → Experiment ✅ → Selection ❌ (committee never met)
Change attempt 2 (process improvement): Fork ✅ → Experiment ❌ (no budget for pilot)
Change attempt 3 (partnership proposal): Fork ✅ → Experiment ✅ → Selection ❌ (no decision criteria)

**Pattern**: Selection is the bottleneck. Two out of three changes died at Selection — no clear decision process, no meeting, no criteria. The fix is not "more ideas" (Fork) or "better pilots" (Experiment). It's "define how decisions get made."
