# Architecture — Evolution Layer

## Function: System Self-Improvement

The Evolution Layer is where the system changes itself.

This is the meta-layer — it operates on the other three layers.

---

## Core Questions

- How does the system discover what needs to change?
- How are changes proposed, tested, and adopted?
- How does the system prevent self-destruction through change?
- How does the system's capacity to evolve itself evolve?

---

## Components

### Variation Generation

Mechanisms for creating new possibilities:
- Random mutation (trial and error)
- Recombination (remixing existing elements)
- Design (intentional creation)
- AI generation (automated variation)

### Selection Mechanisms

Filters that determine what survives:
- Natural selection (environmental fitness)
- Artificial selection (human choice)
- Market selection (user adoption, revenue)
- Computational selection (automated testing, benchmarks)

### Integration

Processes for incorporating successful changes:
- Gradual rollout (canary, A/B testing)
- Backward compatibility
- Migration paths
- Deprecation and sunset

### Meta-Evolution

The evolution layer evolving itself:
- Improving the variation mechanism
- Refining the selection criteria
- Accelerating the integration pipeline

---

## The Role of AI

AI transforms the Evolution Layer by:

1. **Automating variation** — generating more and better candidates
2. **Accelerating selection** — evaluating candidates faster and more accurately
3. **Enabling meta-evolution** — optimizing the evolution process itself

---

## Properties of a Healthy Evolution Layer

- **Active** — the system is continuously generating and testing variations
- **Safe** — changes can be tested without destroying the system
- **Inclusive** — variations can come from anywhere
- **Self-aware** — the evolution process can evolve itself
