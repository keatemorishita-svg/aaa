# Model: AI as Civilization Infrastructure

## The Shift

Before AI, the Evolution Loop required human cognition at every stage:

- Humans had to notice opportunities to fork
- Humans had to design and implement mutations
- Humans had to evaluate and select
- Humans had to integrate changes

AI changes the speed, scale, and cost of every stage.

---

## AI as Evolution Accelerator

### Fork
- AI can identify divergence opportunities faster
- AI can generate candidate forks autonomously
- Cost of exploration drops toward zero

### Experiment
- AI can generate mutations at scale
- AI agents can run experiments continuously
- Hypothesis space expands dramatically

### Selection
- AI can evaluate candidates faster and more consistently
- AI can detect patterns humans miss
- Selection becomes more meritocratic and less political

### Merge
- AI can resolve conflicts intelligently
- AI can maintain compatibility automatically
- Integration friction drops

---

## Two Views of AI's Role

### Weak View (Conservative)
AI is an **evolution accelerator** — it makes the existing loop faster and more efficient. The system still requires human intent, judgment, and values.

### Strong View (Speculative)
AI becomes an **independent evolution unit** — it generates variations, selects among them, and integrates changes without human involvement. The system evolves autonomously.

---

## Current Evidence

The weak view is demonstrated daily:
- AI-generated code → human review → merge
- AI-generated science hypotheses → human experiment → publication
- AI content generation → human curation → distribution

The strong view is not yet demonstrated.

---

## Implication

Whether weak or strong, the direction is clear:

> AI is moving from being a tool used by the system to being part of the system's evolution infrastructure.

The question is not whether this happens, but how we design the interface between human and AI in the evolution loop.
